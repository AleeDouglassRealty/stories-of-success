The Power of Preparation and Timing

How strategic positioning, early exposure, and skilled negotiation led to an exceptional luxury condo sale.

Read the full story
Altura Bayshore
Tampa Bay • Luxury condo market Planned list price: $2,275,000 Sold: $2,435,000

When my clients decided it was time to sell their residence at Altura Bayshore, we knew we were entering a challenging luxury condo market. Similar residences in the building had been averaging nearly 240 days on the market, and we were preparing to launch at $2,275,000 based on current market conditions.

Like most sellers, their goal was simple: achieve the best possible price with the least amount of stress and uncertainty.

The setup required strategy from the start.

Before launching on the MLS, I focused on making sure the residence was positioned for success from day one.

We carefully and thoughtfully prepared the home for sale, developed a pricing and marketing strategy, and hosted a pre-market broker’s open to generate early interest among agents who specialize in luxury properties.

The day before we were scheduled to go live, an agent attended the broker’s open with clients who had been specifically looking for a residence in Altura. They immediately recognized the opportunity.

What happened next came down to preparation, market knowledge, and negotiation.

This is where timing and negotiation make the difference.

The buyers understood what made this residence special. My sellers and I also understood something important: they were not simply interested in the property; they wanted this property. As an experienced business owner and negotiator himself, my seller recognized the same opportunity I did.

We had intentionally priced the home based on current market conditions, but the level of interest we were seeing before launch reinforced our belief that this residence would stand out. With the property scheduled to hit the MLS the following day, the buyers faced the possibility of increased competition and multiple offers.

Rather than rushing to accept the first offer, we strategically created urgency around the opportunity in front of them. By understanding the buyers’ motivation, the uniqueness of the residence, and the leverage created by our upcoming market launch, we were able to negotiate a sale price that exceeded our planned list price by $160,000.

The result was even better than anticipated

The property went under contract before ever hitting the MLS.

The final sale price was $2,435,000, which was $160,000 higher than our planned list price and the highest-priced non-penthouse sale in Altura Bayshore within the previous 12 months.

Even more importantly, my clients avoided months of showings, uncertainty, and market time while achieving an exceptional result.

And that is why preparation matters.

People often think real estate success comes from putting a sign in the yard and getting a property online.

In reality, the best outcomes are often created long before a listing goes live.

Preparation, strategic positioning, professional relationships, and skilled negotiation can make a significant difference in both price and experience. This sale is a great example of how the right buyer, combined with the right strategy, can create an opportunity that benefits everyone involved.

Every home and every seller’s situation is unique. My goal is always to help clients maximize their opportunities through thoughtful preparation, market expertise, and strong negotiation.
